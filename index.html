The Australian government is on the verge of ordering your ISP to track and record your movements online. These plans are made on the pretext of fighting terrorism, but they really belong in a police state. Mandatory data retention policies treat every single citizen as a suspect worthy of constant intrusive surveillance.

This website hopes to provide you with the information you need to be informed and tools to contact your representatives to let them know how you feel about this issue. With your help, we can stop the spies.

Your privacy is under attack

Your personal life, exposed

Under the proposed data retention laws, details about everything you do online or on your phone would be compulsorily collected and stored.

Information about the apps you use, the emails you send, and even the places you go and who you communicate with—all of this data will be collected.

Why you should be worried

It's only data, right?

By piecing together information about your online life from multiple sources, the government plans to use your ISP to build a secret online dossier on every Australian.

Even if this information were only being collected to fight terrorism—and this is far from clear—there remains a frightening risk that it will be misused or fall into the wrong hands.

DATA RETENTION AROUND THE WORLD

AUSTRALIA IS FLOUTING HUMAN RIGHTS

The Court of Justice of the European Union this year ruled a similar European data retention mandate as illegal, being "an interference with the fundamental rights of practically the entire European population."

The United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights also recently criticised mandatory data retention rules as being neither necessary nor proportionate.

Who's paying for all this?

The Australian public

Australia’s second-largest ISP has estimated the cost of the mandatory data retention proposal at an extra $130 per customer, per year.

Here are some talking points you can use

I oppose mandatory data retention in Australia. As my representative, I'm asking you to reject this proposal.

I value my digital privacy. When I use the Internet, I want secure, private communication. I don't want to be surveilled and spied on when I'm not suspected of a crime.

The proposal would push all the costs of data retention onto Internet Service Providers, who would pass the costs onto their customers. That means higher bills for Australians, as well as higher costs for new ISPs looking to enter the market.

Courts around the world are finding data retention mandates unconstitutional because they violate individual privacy rights. Let's do the right thing in Australia and stop these proposals before they become the law.
